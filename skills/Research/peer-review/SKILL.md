# Peer Review

Systematic peer review toolkit. Evaluate methodology, statistics, design, reproducibility, ethics, figure integrity, reporting standards, for manuscript and grant review across disciplines.

## Instruction
- Identify the specific review task, whether it is an academic manuscript, a grant proposal, or a conference submission.
- Evaluate the research methodology, checking for appropriateness of design, datasets, and variables.
- Review statistical analysis and results, assessing significance, effect sizes, and reporting standards.
- Assess reproducibility, ethics, and figure integrity to ensure scientific rigor.
- Synthesize findings into a formal review report with clear strengths, weaknesses, and actionable recommendations.

## When to Use
- When conducting systematic peer reviews for scientific journals or funding agencies.
- When evaluating the quality and reproducibility of manuscripts or grant applications across disciplines.

## Output
- A structured peer review report with categorized feedback (Methodology, Results, Ethics, etc.).
- Actionable suggestions for authors or researchers to improve the quality of their work.
