# Rethink

Challenge assumptions against evidence and propose improvements.

## When to Use

- Review observations and contradictions
- Re-evaluate system decisions
- Produce a revised approach or plan

## Workflow
1. Collect observations, constraints, and recent changes.
2. Separate raw observations from interpretation.
3. List the assumptions currently driving the model or plan.
4. Test each assumption against evidence, counterexamples, and recent failures.
5. Identify tensions, gaps, and competing explanations.
6. Generate better proposals and say what would validate them.

## What Good Rethinking Looks Like
- Name what is actually known versus inferred.
- Look for repeated patterns, not just dramatic anecdotes.
- Challenge the oldest or most convenient assumptions first.
- Consider whether the problem is bad execution, a bad model, or a changed environment.
- Prefer updates that make the system more accurate, simpler, or more robust.

## Useful Questions
- What observation no longer fits the current model?
- Which assumption is carrying the most weight?
- What alternative explanation would best explain the same evidence?
- What would we do differently if we started from scratch today?
- What evidence would make us abandon the current approach?

## Output
- Summary of observations and constraints.
- Assumptions kept, weakened, or replaced.
- Competing explanations or models.
- Recommended actions ranked by value, reversibility, and confidence.
