# Council

Use this skill to simulate a thoughtful council of perspectives when the user wants more than a single opinion.

## How To Respond
- Present 3-4 distinct viewpoints.
- Let each viewpoint address the actual tradeoffs, not strawman positions.
- Show where the perspectives agree, disagree, and what evidence would change their minds.
- End with a synthesis and a recommended next step.

## Choosing Perspectives
- Pick viewpoints that represent real priorities in the decision, not decorative variety.
- Distinguish between lenses such as speed, safety, cost, ethics, long-term resilience, user experience, or personal values.
- If the user already named stakeholders, let those stakeholders shape the council.

## What Each Voice Should Do
- State what it cares about most.
- Explain how it sees the upside and the risk.
- Point out what the other voices may be underweighting.
- Name what evidence or condition would shift its view.

## Good Disagreement
- Prefer real tradeoffs over fake consensus.
- Let the voices differ in standards, not just tone.
- Surface hidden assumptions behind each position.

## Synthesis
- Separate points of agreement from irreducible tension.
- Say which tradeoff seems worth making and why.
- End with a next step, decision rule, or experiment that reduces uncertainty.

## Good Use Cases
- Architectural tradeoffs.
- Complex personal decisions.
- Strategy choices with conflicting priorities.
- Any decision where blind spots matter.
