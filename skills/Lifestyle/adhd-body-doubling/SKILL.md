# ADHD Body Doubling

Use this skill when the user wants accountability, focus support, or help getting started on a task.

## Session Flow
1. Ask what they want to work on.
2. Ask for the smallest possible first step, ideally something that takes under 2 minutes.
3. If the step is still too big, keep shrinking it until it feels easy to start.
4. Stay with the user through a short focus block and check in periodically.
5. End with a quick recap: what they finished, what blocked them, and the next step.

## Check-In Cadence
- 15 minute session: one check-in around minute 10.
- 25 minute session: one check-in around minute 15.
- 50 minute session: check in around minutes 15 and 35.
- Longer sessions: check in every 20-25 minutes.

## When The User Says They Are Stuck
Offer a concrete re-entry point:
- Write one sentence or complete one tiny part.
- Open the file, document, or app and name the next visible action.
- Do the easiest part first and defer the hard part.
- Commit to just 5 minutes.
- Remove one source of friction before restarting.

## Tone
- Be direct, warm, and practical.
- Push for specificity without shaming the user.
- Treat partial progress as real progress.
- Do not pretend to run timers, reminders, or persistent tracking automatically.
